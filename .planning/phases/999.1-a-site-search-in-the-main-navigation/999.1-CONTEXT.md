# Phase 999.1 — Site search in main navigation (backlog)

## Intent

Visitors should be able to **find exhibitions, artists, and practical visit information** without hunting through the whole homepage. A **search entry point in the main navigation** makes discovery explicit and matches expectations from large museum and cultural sites.

## Scope (when promoted)

- **Placement:** Persistent search affordance in the **primary nav** (desktop and mobile), consistent with the dual-mode shell (Planning / On-site).
- **Search surface:** Likely a **modal, drawer, or dedicated search route** opened from the nav trigger — exact pattern TBD in design.
- **Content targets (candidates):** exhibition titles, artist names, tags, static pages (hours, tickets, directions), and possibly collection objects if the IA expands later.

## Dual-mode note

- **Planning mode:** Search probably emphasizes **what’s on**, tickets, and planning content.
- **On-site mode:** Search might **prioritize** current exhibitions, floor or room hints, or audio-guide links — or stay unified with different ranking; **needs a product decision** when this leaves backlog.

## Prototype constraints (Kunsthaus v1)

- The competition prototype is **static-first** (JSON content, no CMS). A realistic v1 could use **client-side search** over bundled JSON, or a **placeholder UI** with fake results until a backend exists.
- Full **typeahead, typo tolerance, and analytics** are likely **post–v1** unless scope allows.

## Open questions

1. Mandatory for competition deliverable or **nice-to-have** if time remains?
2. **Mobile:** icon-only vs field-in-nav vs full-width overlay?
3. **Accessibility:** focus trap, live region for results, keyboard navigation — fold into phase plan when scheduled.
4. **i18n:** German / English parity for labels and results (align with rest of site).
