# Phase 999.1 — Site search in main navigation (backlog)

## Intent

Visitors should be able to **find exhibitions, artists, and practical visit information** without hunting through the whole homepage. A **search entry point in the main navigation** makes discovery explicit and matches expectations from large museum and cultural sites.

## Research note (generic vs. Kunsthaus)

The **museum-sector patterns** below come from **aggregated website analytics, user-behavior studies, and conference literature** (e.g. Museums and the Web; museum UX surveys summarized in academic and trade publishing). They are **not** Kunsthaus Zürich–specific.

**Before locking scope or IA**, complement this with **Kunsthaus Zürich research**: review **kunsthaus.ch** content and navigation, competition prototype goals, any available analytics, and (if possible) **short stakeholder or visitor interviews** so priorities match this institution—not only sector defaults.

## Museum-sector context: three common intent clusters

Across large museum sites, observed needs often group into:

1. **Planning a physical visit** — logistics, tickets, timing, “what should I see?”
2. **Exploring the online collection** — objects, artists, highlights, browsing paths
3. **Specialized research** — deeper facts, provenance, scholarship, repeat visits after an on-site experience

The main-nav search concept should eventually **support or signpost** these clusters where the site has matching content—even if the v1 prototype only implements a subset.

## Query and content types often seen on museum sites

| Area | Examples (sector-level) |
|------|-------------------------|
| **Specific artworks & objects** | Famous or “must-see” works (e.g. institution-specific highlights named in marketing) |
| **Thematic or topical** | Genres, subjects, periods (“portrait,” “landscape,” a given century) |
| **Artists, movements, mediums** | Named artists; materials or techniques (“painting,” “sculpture”) |
| **Practical visit information** | Hours, location/directions, admission, parking — especially **before** a planned visit |
| **What’s on** | Current/upcoming **temporary exhibitions**, programs, special events |
| **Educational resources** | Teacher packs, curriculum ties, online learning (where offered) |
| **High-resolution images & detail** | Zoom, download, inspiration use (designers, press, engaged visitors) |

Use this table as a **checklist for future content indexing** and result types—not as a promise that the Kunsthaus prototype indexes every row.

## Visitor search behaviors (sector-level)

- **External search vs. on-site search:** A large share of traffic to **online collection** experiences arrives via **external search engines** (e.g. Google) rather than the museum’s own search box—so **on-site search** must still be excellent for people who *do* start from the homepage/nav, while **SEO and landing pages** remain the main acquisition path for collection discovery. Exact percentages vary by institution; treat “~75% from organic” as **illustrative**, not a Kunsthaus metric.
- **Inspiration vs. information:** Some sessions are **broad and visual** (“browse, feel, get ideas”); others are **narrow and factual** (specific work, date, room). UI and empty states should not assume only one mode.
- **Before and after a visit:** **Pre-visit** behavior skews toward **logistics and highlights**; **post-visit** often skews toward **re-finding an object**, sharing, or **deeper research**. Dual-mode (Planning / On-site) may map partly onto this rhythm but is **not** identical—validate for Kunsthaus.

## Scope (when promoted)

- **Placement:** Persistent search affordance in the **primary nav** (desktop and mobile), consistent with the dual-mode shell (Planning / On-site).
- **Search surface:** Likely a **modal, drawer, or dedicated search route** opened from the nav trigger — exact pattern TBD in design.
- **Content targets (candidates):** Align with sector patterns above **where** the prototype has data: exhibition titles, artists, tags, visit pages, and—if in scope—collection objects. **Kunsthaus-specific prioritization** comes from local research.

## Dual-mode note

- **Planning mode:** Likely emphasizes **what’s on**, tickets, and **practical visit** answers (matches pre-visit sector behavior).
- **On-site mode:** May **prioritize** current exhibitions, wayfinding or room-level hints, or post-visit “find again” paths—or stay **unified** with different ranking; **needs product decisions** informed by Kunsthaus context, not sector generalizations alone.

## Prototype constraints (Kunsthaus v1)

- The competition prototype is **static-first** (JSON content, no CMS). A realistic v1 could use **client-side search** over bundled JSON, or a **placeholder UI** with fake results until a backend exists.
- Full **typeahead, typo tolerance, and analytics** are likely **post–v1** unless scope allows.
