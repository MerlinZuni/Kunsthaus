# Creative Briefing - Kunsthaus Zürich

## Kunsthaus Zürich Digital Platform Pitch

**For:** Selected Liip Designers
**Date:** 29 March 2026

---

## The Project

- **Problem:** Today's fragmented ecosystem creates disconnected journeys and limits editorial flexibility.
- **Competition:** Dreipol is our main competitor, they already created the app and have a headstart in the tech stack (read cheaper offer).
- **Opportunity:** Kunsthaus Zürich is redefining its digital presence as a unified, future-ready platform to connect content, visit planning, and long-term audience engagement, moving past the current fragmented ecosystem.
- **Insight:** Thinking out of the box, pushing the boundaries of what is expected is our only route to win, upselling and new revenue streams digitally is what the client wants.
- **Project Plan:** Broken into two phases, 1) MVP by the end of year, and 2) an expansion afterwards.
- **Scope:** As designers, you each have 8 hours to complete this brief.

## Liip's Strategy

The website is not one experience. It's a system of modes across time.

### Experience Framework

**Three core dimensions:**
- Information / Trust — clarity, credibility, orientation
- Inspiration / Emotion — visual impact, curiosity, engagement
- Education / Depth — deeper learning, retention, cultural value

**Three user states:**
- Before the visit → inspire and guide
- During the visit → support and orient
- After the visit → extend and deepen

Interface design consideration for shifting between these modes.

## The Challenge

> How might we translate the richness, relevance, and emotional impact of the Kunsthaus into a digital experience that feels as compelling as the museum?

This is not only about usability, but about engagement with art/culture, & the institution over time.

The design must successfully balance emotional storytelling and inspiration with usability and functional clarity.

The design must be modular, flexible and easily applicable to all future exhibitions. They're planning a complete rebranding in around 2 years. So, the design we're suggesting is a temporary solution.

### Ambition / Objectives for Digital Experience

- Platform as a central entry point to the Kunsthaus ecosystem
- Connects inspiration, exploration, visit planning, and retention into one continuous journey
- Balances cultural depth with accessibility and clarity
- Enables conversion (tickets, membership, events) without feeling transactional
- Works seamlessly across mobile contexts, supporting users before, during, after visit modes
- Meets accessibility standards (WCAG) by design
- Platform extends the physical museum experience into digital (not just represent it)
- Experience actively motivates visits and sharing (not just inform)
- Create a sense of the museum "before arrival" (anticipation & atmosphere)

## Audience / Broad and Diverse

The audience is diverse, ranging from casual, mobile-first explorers to highly engaged art audiences. Groups include:
- First-time/returning visitors
- Members
- Local/international users requiring multilingual support (DE/EN/FR)
- **Challenge:** Design should balance low barriers to entry with depth and discoverability.

## Key Experience Moments / Consider Journeys

### Before visit
- **Exploration** — Connecting exhibitions, collection, and themes
- **Inspiration** — Attracting attention through content and storytelling
- **Activation** — Guiding toward tickets, events, membership

### On-site (orientation & contextual support deeper content)
- **Visit Support** — Enabling planning and on-site enhancement

### After visit (reflection & retention)
- **Retention** — Creating reasons to return and stay connected

## Distinct Creative Direction (Open Territory)

- How a museum becomes a living, digital cultural space (inspiration)
- The interplay of art, typography, and interface
- How digital design can evoke curation, atmosphere, and discovery (emotion)

## Content Perspective — Move from Academic to Storytelling

- Editorial formats (stories, essays, interviews)
- Connections between exhibitions, collection, and program
- A more narrative, contextual, and engaging content experience

### Content Priority Hierarchy

1. Inspiration
2. Key information (opening hours, CTA ticket booking)
3. Current exhibitions (teasing)
4. Museum offering (shop, membership, etc.) → focus on cross-selling
5. Events & programs
6. Planning details (visit on site)
7. Educational deep dives (content highlight)

## Functional Considerations

You do not need to solve systems, just show awareness and consideration.

- Ticketing & membership journeys
- Event discovery and calendar navigation
- Integration of collection and archival content
- Scalable, modular content structures (Headless CMS / editorial flexibility planned)
- Current system landscape ecosystem (Smeetz, collection database, Visitor Guide, Digi Lab)
- On-site priorities: Orientation, Exhibit-related content, Practical services

## Design Principles (Guiding, Not Limiting)

- **Kunsthaus Mission Statement:** A place for living encounters with art, committed to artistic freedom. It is independent and open to all.
- **About Kunsthaus:** We show art that excites, moves, challenges, and expands our horizons: art of compellingly unique power.
- Design Direction & Narrative anchoring tools
  - Consider writing your own Design Purpose: Why the web platform exists
  - Consider writing Design Principles: Key values+definitions that guide your choices.
- Prioritise progressive disclosure (from inspiration → into detail)
- Tricky: Balance emotional storytelling with immediate clarity

- 1 strong concept direction
- A focused moodboard / visual territory
- A homepage design expressing the idea

Concepts will be evaluated on:
- Strength and clarity of creative idea
- Quality of storytelling and narrative thinking
- Visual distinctiveness and cultural relevance
